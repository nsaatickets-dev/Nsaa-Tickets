# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two buyer contexts, roughly equal weight:
- **Mobile-first, on-the-go**: buying last-minute on a phone, often via Mobile Money, sometimes on patchy data. Needs speed and clarity over exhaustive detail.
- **Planning-ahead**: buying in advance for a specific event (wedding, conference, concert), comparing options, wants confidence in legitimacy and fee transparency before committing.

Both are in Ghana, across a wide range of event types (concerts, nightlife, conferences, sports, weddings, comedy, theatre, religious events, workshops).

## Product Purpose

A guest-first, fee-transparent, fraud-resistant ticketing marketplace for Ghana. Core workflow is browse → buy → scan: discover an event, complete guest checkout (name + phone, no account wall) within a 10-minute inventory hold, receive a signed one-time-use QR ticket, and have it scanned at the door. Success looks like buyers trusting the platform enough to complete checkout under time pressure, and organizers trusting it enough to avoid resale/fraud.

## Brand Personality

Confident & premium, local & culturally grounded, calm & clear. Modern, not dull. Upscale without being cold — should still carry the energy of the events themselves, not read as a sterile fintech dashboard.

## Anti-references

- Generic Eventbrite/ticket-site template — no distinct identity.
- Informal resale/WhatsApp-flyer aesthetic — this product is explicitly positioned against that culture.
- Generic dark "crypto/SaaS" template (black bg, gold/purple accent, glassmorphism) — worth flagging explicitly since the current CSS tokens (`nsaa.css`) already use a dark bg + gold accent; future design work should push the palette toward something distinctly NSAA/Ghanaian rather than the AI-generated-site cliché this superficially resembles.
- Anything that reads as dull or generic rather than modern.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust is the primary conversion lever.** Every screen should visibly reinforce legitimacy — itemized fees, ticket authenticity, payment security — ahead of pure aesthetics.
2. **Calm under time pressure.** Checkout runs against a real 10-minute countdown; hierarchy and pacing should reduce anxiety, never add to it.
3. **Locally authentic, not template-local.** Ghanaian identity should be genuine in typography, imagery, tone, and payment framing (Mobile Money) — not a Western SaaS shell with local copy pasted in.
4. **Premium without being cold.** Confident and upscale, but warm/energetic enough to match the "gathering" emotion of the events being sold.
5. **Product-first register.** Marketing/discovery pages (home, category, event, about) set the tone, but every design decision should ultimately serve the browse → buy → scan workflow.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: solid contrast, full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, readable type at all sizes.
