Cookie Policy

Last updated: 9 July 2026

Not a substitute for legal advice

This page describes the actual cookies and local storage Nsaa sets today. It has not been reviewed by qualified legal counsel and should be re-confirmed whenever a new provider or analytics tool is added.

What we use today

Nsaa uses browser local storage (not a cookie) to remember your most recent order so you can find your tickets again, and a small local flag to remember you've dismissed the cookie notice. If you sign in, our authentication provider, Clerk, sets its own session cookies/tokens to keep you signed in. No advertising or third-party tracking cookies are set at this time.

Essential vs. optional

Authentication and checkout-related storage are essential to the service and cannot be disabled without breaking sign-in and order lookup. Nothing on Nsaa today is optional/advertising related; any future analytics or marketing cookies would be optional and disclosed here before being added.

Managing cookies

You can clear cookies and local storage through your browser settings at any time; this may sign you out or clear your last-order shortcut.
